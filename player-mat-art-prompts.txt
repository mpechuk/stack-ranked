STACK RANKED — PLAYER MAT ART GENERATION PROMPTS
==================================================
Two image-generation prompts for the optional decorative art on the Player
Desk Mat (see generate_player_mat.py): a HEADER BANNER that sits in the top
band next to the mat title, and a full-bleed BACKGROUND TEXTURE that sits
behind every tracker on the mat. Both are optional — the mat renders fine
with neither (a plain placeholder box / flat cream fill is used instead).

Save the generated files as:
  mat-images/player-mat-header.png       (HEADER BANNER prompt below)
  mat-images/player-mat-background.png   (BACKGROUND TEXTURE prompt below)

MASTER STYLE (same family as card-art-prompts.txt, so the whole
print-and-play kit — box cover, cards, and mat — reads as one product):
  Retro corporate-propaganda poster art, matching the box cover illustration
  (see docs/CoverV2.png) — bold black ink outlines, halftone screentone
  shading, worn paper-grain texture, dramatic radiating sunburst linework,
  satirical heroic composition, screen-print poster look. Base palette of
  burnt orange, cream, and navy. No text, no logos, no watermarks — this is
  illustration only, the mat's own labels/trackers are added separately by
  the PDF generator on top.

Render at high resolution (300 DPI at print size) so the PDF stays crisp:
  Header banner: ~2550 x 480 px  (fills a ~5:1 landscape band)
  Background texture: ~3300 x 2550 px (fills the full 11in x 8.5in mat)


================================================================
HEADER BANNER — mat-images/player-mat-header.png
================================================================
REFERENCE: Sits to the right of the navy "STACK RANKED / PLAYER DESK MAT"
title plate, across the top of the mat. Same illustration family as the box
cover (a duo triumphantly climbing a junk-office mountain into a sunrise) —
this crop should feel like a continuation of that scene, not a new one.

PROMPT:
Scene: The same climbers from the Stack Ranked box cover — one hoisting a
steaming coffee cup, the other a briefcase — now shown further along their
ascent, striding across a wide ridge of stacked filing cabinets, monitors,
and tangled cables toward a radiant sunrise on the horizon. Cropped as a
wide horizontal banner focused on the mid-distance skyline and the
sunburst, with the climbers as small triumphant silhouettes rather than the
main focus (this crop needs to read well as a thin strip, not a portrait).
Style: retro corporate-propaganda poster art (matching the box cover) —
bold black ink outlines, halftone screentone shading, worn paper grain,
radiating sunburst background, screen-print poster look. Palette: burnt
orange, cream, and navy. Format: wide landscape banner crop (~5:1), no
text/logos/watermarks.


================================================================
BACKGROUND TEXTURE — mat-images/player-mat-background.png
================================================================
REFERENCE: Sits behind every tracker, zone, and label on the mat — printed
text and ink-outlined boxes are drawn directly on top of it, so this has to
stay quiet and pale rather than being a full illustration.

PROMPT:
Scene: A subtle, mostly-empty aged cream paper background — faint worn
paper-grain texture, a few very light halftone screentone patches, and
extremely faint radiating sunburst linework fading in from one corner
(echoing the box cover's sunrise), with a couple of small, low-contrast
corporate-satire doodles (a tiny stapler, a coiled phone cord, a stack of
paper) tucked unobtrusively near the edges, never in the center.
Style: same retro corporate-propaganda poster family as the box cover, but
rendered at very low contrast and high brightness overall — think "the box
cover art, bleached down to 10-15% strength" — so black printed text and
thin ink-outlined boxes placed on top stay fully legible everywhere on the
page, including dead center. No strong dark shapes, no busy detail, no
text/logos/watermarks. Palette: pale cream and warm off-white, with only
whispers of burnt orange and navy. Format: landscape, US Letter proportions
(~1.3:1), edge-to-edge (no border/frame baked in — the PDF adds its own).
